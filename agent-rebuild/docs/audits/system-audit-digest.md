## 3. Top 10 most important insights from the audit pack

These are the most important conclusions after re-reading the pack.

---

## 1. The business is viable, but only if operational truth gets fixed

The business is not dead, but it is partially blind.
The pack repeatedly shows that the major constraints are not lack of opportunity — they are:
- bad visibility
- broken payment truth
- inconsistent follow-up
- stale board debt
- uneven operational execution

This means the ceiling is operational, not market-size.

---

## 2. Back Market is structurally important and already the most automated domain

Back Market is a major revenue engine and the audit pack shows it already has the clearest scriptable operating model.
That matters because it proves the future architecture:
- deterministic operational flows can be scripted
- the agent should sit above that for judgement, not wrap every step

This is the strongest proof-of-concept domain in the company.

---

## 3. Payment truth is one of the biggest unresolved system problems

Across the pack, finance/payment mapping remains one of the biggest weak spots.
The major theme is:
- money may be captured
- but the business cannot always reliably connect that back to jobs, invoices, and true operating state

This contaminates:
- channel profitability
- AR understanding
- revenue reporting
- decision confidence

The pack strongly supports making payment reconciliation one of the first rebuild priorities.

---

## 4. Customer-service follow-up failure is a direct revenue leak

The customer-retention and Intercom-related work clearly shows:
- slow or absent reply behaviour
- paid-but-not-contacted risk
- poor continuity between booking, repair status, and communication

This is not just a CX issue.
It is a revenue and churn issue.

---

## 5. Monday contains valuable truth but also heavy operational noise

The audit pack repeatedly surfaces that Monday is both:
- the best available operating record
- and a polluted system with stale items, inconsistent updates, and workflow ambiguity

So Monday should be treated as:
- an important source
- but not unquestioned truth

It needs deterministic cleanup, validators, and shadow reporting surfaces.

---

## 6. There is real SEO and demand opportunity hidden in current data

The GSC, product-card, and profitability work shows that iCorrect already has:
- meaningful demand across repair categories
- profitable products with visible demand
- missing Shopify listings that should exist
- low-margin/high-demand products that need repricing or withdrawal

This means marketing should not operate from intuition only.
A measurable demand × margin operating layer is possible.

---

## 7. The product catalogue has pricing and listing problems, not just traffic problems

The audit pack makes clear that performance is not only a traffic issue.
It is also a catalogue discipline issue:
- profitable things missing from Shopify
- low-margin or negative-margin repairs left live
- price mismatches
- weak linkage between search demand and offer surface

So “marketing” and “pricing/catalog” are not separable in practice.

---

## 8. Team performance can be measured much more concretely than current management rhythm suggests

The staff-performance and Monday activity work shows the raw ingredients already exist to build:
- completions by person
- work-in-progress burden
- inactivity / gap patterns
- queue ownership visibility
- likely coaching / capacity insights

The business is currently under-using the data it already has.

---

## 9. There are real logistics / supplier / parts opportunities, but they need a proper deterministic layer

The pack’s supplier/logistics and parts-related work points to a familiar pattern:
- stock, supply costs, supplier economics, and usage patterns are important
- but they are not yet surfaced in a consistently operational way

This supports a parts-system rebuild with scripts first, agent second.

---

## 10. The audit pack already implies the right future architecture: scripts for truth, agents for interpretation

Even though the pack was not written as a pure architecture document, the conclusion emerges very clearly:
- deterministic pulls, matching, ranking, reconciliation, triage, and summaries should be scripts
- agents should interpret, write, prioritise, and recommend

That is the biggest meta-insight of the entire pack.
