Walk-In Intake Process v2

Phase 1 Pilot | 9 March 2026

The Problem

With no dedicated front-of-house, every walk-in pulls a technician off the bench. That costs us repairs, revenue, and output. The goal: minimise time away from the bench, capture everything properly, and make sure nothing gets missed.

The New Flow

1

Customer Arrives

Same as today. Customer walks in, fills in the Typeform on the iPad. The form asks why they're here: drop-off (booked), drop-off (walk-in), collection, or enquiry.

↓
2

Smarter Form Questions

NEW

We're adding questions so we capture more before anyone goes upstairs. Less discovery, more confirmation.

Has your device had any liquid damage or been dropped?
Yes / No / Not sure
Is the data on your device backed up?
Yes / No / I don't know
Would you like your device delivered back after repair (free of charge)?
Deliver to me / I'll collect in person
We will need your device passcode for testing. Please have it ready.
Acknowledged (not asking them to type it, just a heads-up)
Do you have any other questions before we book your device in?
Free text (optional)

For customers who haven't reviewed pricing online, the form will show pricing information before these questions.

↓
3

Slack Notification + Agent Briefing

NEW

Typeform submits, Slack notification lands (same as today). But now an agent automatically replies in the thread with extra context pulled from Monday and Intercom.

Returning customer. Last repair: MacBook Air screen replacement, Oct 2025.
Intercom: 3 previous conversations. Last one: battery health query, Jan 2026.
Monday: No open items.
Note: Customer has NOT reviewed pricing online. Confirm pricing before booking in.

Andres reads this before going upstairs. He already knows who the customer is and what they need.

↓
4

Andres Sees the Customer

Andres goes up with context. The conversation is now confirmation, not discovery:

  • Confirm the device and issue
  • Take the passcode verbally
  • Take payment if needed
  • Collect the device

Target: 3-5 minutes, not 10-15.

↓
5

Voice Note

NEW

Back downstairs, Andres leaves a voice note in the Slack thread. No typing. Just talk for 30 seconds.

"Customer John, MacBook Pro 14 M1 Pro, dropped it last week, screen cracked on the left side, no AppleCare, wants delivery back, took £49 deposit on SumUp, passcode 4521."

The voice note is automatically transcribed and added as an update to the Monday.com item. Andres talks, the system writes.

↓
6

Monday.com Updated Automatically

The repair item in Monday now has everything:

  • Customer details from the form (name, email, phone, device, issue)
  • New form answers (liquid damage, backup, delivery preference)
  • Agent context (prior repairs, Intercom history)
  • Andres's voice note, transcribed and formatted

The technician picking up the repair has full context. No chasing, no guessing.

Before

  • Andres goes up blind, 10-15 min per walk-in
  • Intake notes inconsistent or missing
  • No prior customer context at intake
  • Passcode chased after the fact
  • Every customer comes back to collect
  • "Issue: Slow" with no detail

After

  • Andres has a briefing, 3-5 min per walk-in
  • Voice note captures everything automatically
  • Agent pulls Monday + Intercom history
  • Customer told to have passcode ready
  • Delivery option offered upfront
  • Form captures damage, backup, and more

What Comes Next (Phase 2)

  • Replace Typeform with a custom intake form (saves £70/month, dynamic questions per device)
  • Enquiry routing to Ferrari via video call for complex questions
  • New Technical Operations Coordinator takes over front of house
  • AI-powered intake that answers common questions before escalating
