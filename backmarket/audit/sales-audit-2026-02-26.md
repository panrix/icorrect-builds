### Purchase Price: BM Offer vs Monday

The BM Buyback API reports an `originalPrice` (e.g. £129) while Monday records a lower "Purchase Price (ex VAT)" (e.g. £117). Cross-referencing 39 items:

- **36 of 39**: Monday purchase = BM offer ÷ 1.1 (exact match)
- **3 outliers**: BM 1126 (ratio 1.94), BM 1370 (ratio 1.0), BM 1239 (ratio 2.57) — likely counter-offers or manual corrections

**iCorrect's actual cost is the BM offer price** (£129 in this example). The Monday column records the ex-VAT figure (£117). The difference (£12) is VAT, not a BM commission.

This means all profit calculations using Monday purchase prices are using ex-VAT figures. The actual cash outflow per trade-in is ~10% higher than what Monday shows.

| | BM 1474 example |
|---|---|

| Monday "Purchase Price (ex VAT)" | **£117** |
| Difference | £12 (VAT) |
| Customer receives | £117 |

### Complete Trade-In → Sale Flow

Mapped from BM 1474 (GB-26084-MQJFV), a completed trade-in with full data:

```
1. CUSTOMER selects condition on BM website
   → aestheticGradeCode: FUNCTIONAL_CRACKED
   → BM shows offer: £129

2. BM assigns internal grade after customer submits
   → listing.grade: SILVER
   → listing.sku: MBA13.2020.M1.7CORE.8GB.256GB.FUNC.CRACK

3. CUSTOMER ships device (Royal Mail)
   → tracking: YR686248371GB
   → shipped: 20 Feb → received at iCorrect: 23-24 Feb

4. iCORRECT DIAGNOSES — compares reported vs actual condition:
   | Component     | Customer said | iCorrect found |
   |---------------|---------------|----------------|
   | Battery       | Normal        | Normal         |
   | Screen        | Damaged       | Damaged        |
   | Casing        | Good          | Fair (worse)   |
   | Functionality | Functional    | Functional     |
   | Liquid damage | No            | No             |

5. iCORRECT REPAIRS
   → Parts: LCD - A2337, Charger, Adapters, Cable
   → Diagnostic: 31 min, Refurb: 1h 02m (Tech: Mykhailo)
   → Final grading: Fair

6. LISTED ON BM for sale as "Fair" condition
   → Sale price TBD (not yet listed)

7. PAYMENT FLOW
   → iCorrect pays BM: £129 (BM offer = actual cost)
   → Monday records: £117 (ex VAT)
   → BM pays customer: £117
```

### Three Grade Systems

| System | Where | Values | Purpose |
|--------|-------|--------|---------|
| **Aesthetic grade** | Buyback listing (`aestheticGradeCode`) | FUNCTIONAL_FLAWLESS, FUNCTIONAL_GOOD, FUNCTIONAL_USED, FUNCTIONAL_CRACKED, NOT_FUNCTIONAL_USED, NOT_FUNCTIONAL_CRACKED | Customer self-assessment at trade-in |
| **BM order grade** | Buyback order (`listing.grade`) | DIAMOND, GOLD, SILVER, BRONZE, STALLONE | BM's internal grade after submission |
| **Sales grade** | Sales listing (`grade`) | EXCELLENT, GOOD, FAIR | Final condition for resale after repair |

These are **not 1:1 mappings**. A customer selecting FUNCTIONAL_CRACKED can be graded SILVER or BRONZE by BM. After repair, iCorrect grades it independently (usually Fair for cracked devices).

  - SILVER + MBA M1 + Fair = the specific loss-maker recipe (5 of 8 losses)

### Medium-Term Actions

2. **Shift volume toward higher-margin SKUs:**
  - MBP 14-inch A2442 (55% gross) — actively source more
  - MBA M2 A2681 Midnight (51-59% gross) — growing demand

3. **Monitor the A2681 (M2 MBA) Midnight price** — it's dropped 13% recently. If this follows the M1 MBA pattern, it'll keep dropping.
