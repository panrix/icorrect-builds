## Growth Plans

### Short-term (This Year)
- Get BM revenue to £15k/week (covers all costs)
- Build out corporate pipeline
- Cut low-value repairs (iPhone 7, A1706 screens)
- Implement performance tracking across all roles
- Hire: team assistant, SAF-level tech, operations manager
- Move out of central London office (explore options)

### Medium-term (1-3 Years)
- Scale BM to dedicated account manager level (>£35k/month)
- Build diagnostic MacOS app (proprietary tech)
- Improve connectivity between advanced repairs and lower-level
- Build personal brand / global reputation
- Explore European markets (France, Germany, Spain via Back Market)
- Portugal as potential European hub

### Long-term (3-5 Years)
- US market entry — "the biggest market in the world"
  - More Apple devices per capita
  - More recycled devices
  - One base → 52 states, same infrastructure/language/culture
  - "Back Market haven't taken over the states"
- Performance business (not lifestyle) — intention to sell
- Build IP: diagnostic systems, proprietary data, unique tech
- Target exit: within 5 years before technology advances make current repairs obsolete

### Performance Business vs Lifestyle Business
> Ali: "With a performance business, it goes to IP, systems, technology, assets. That's what gets you the valuation. They want the data that you've actually garnered that they can't get anywhere else in the world."

- Unique advantages to productize:
  - Voltage-based diagnostics (checking voltage to determine faults)
  - Screen flex research data
  - Repair success rate data by model/part
  - Diagnostic system IP
