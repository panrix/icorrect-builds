## Ali's 5A's Framework

Ricky's business advisor Ali uses a structured framework for business transformation. This is the strategic lens through which all iCorrect decisions are made.

### 1. Alignment (End-State Vision)
**Question:** What does perfection look like 3 years from now?

Key decisions to make:
- Lifestyle business or performance business?
- How many employees?
- How much revenue?
- What type of products/services?
- Share vision with team, re-align every 90 days
- Team must be completely bought in

**Status:** Vision exercise with Suzy not yet completed.

### 2. Awarenesses (Insecurities, Concerns, Blindspots)
**Question:** What do you know you need but aren't dealing with?

Current awareness list (from Ali session):
1. Lack of senior management
2. Lack of sales structure/process
3. **Cashflow** (triple-underlined by Ali)
4. Process & systems (SOPs)
5. Inventory management
6. Office location
7. Suppliers
8. Lack of brand awareness
9. Staffing & high quality staff
10. Lack of technical knowledge in team

### 3. Accountabilities (90-Day Ownership)
**Question:** Who owns each big problem for the next 90 days?

Format: Problem → Person responsible
- Each problem needs a single owner
- Owner reports on progress at quarterly review
- If no owner, it doesn't get done

### 4. Activities (3-6 Big Things Per Quarter)
**Question:** What are the 3-6 things that will move the needle this quarter?

Q1 2026 candidates (from Ali session):
1. Website optimisation and SEO (Shopify launch)
2. Internal systems (prep for corporate attack, internal sales processes)
3. Active marketing strategy
4. Capacity to output 110 repairs/month
5. Turn a profit
6. Ability to accurately forecast (financial modelling)

Weekly rhythm: MMM (Monday) sets activities, FAD (Friday) reviews them.

### 5. Assets (Buy, Develop, Improve)
**Question:** What 20-30 investments would improve the business?

Categories:
- **Buy:** New equipment, software, hiring
- **Develop:** Training, new processes, automation
- **Improve:** Existing systems, team skills, supplier relationships

Each investment scored by ROI and prioritised.

"What's the one thing we need to do that, if we did, would make everything else easier or irrelevant?"

**Answer:** Get a fully structured Panrix Project Plan with every project, dependencies, and clear priority order.

### Why This Matters
- Can't hold team accountable without defined work
- Can't sequence automations without knowing the critical path
- External system needed for ADHD management (Ricky's context)
- Hiring/firing clarity requires knowing what work exists
- Improves Claude effectiveness (single source of truth vs archaeology)

### Current State
- Projects scattered across Claude conversations, Monday fragments, and Ricky's head
- No single source of truth
- Ad-hoc task assignment
- Unclear automation sequencing

### Planned Process
1. Voice transcript brain dump (buckets exercise)
2. Extract themes: problems, ideas, goals, dependencies, repeated themes
3. Cluster into discrete projects with clear outcomes
4. Prioritise against actual goals (revenue, time/stress reduction, unlock dependencies, filter distractions)
5. Sequence into timeline (Q1 vs Q2 vs later, this week vs this month)
6. Output: Single working document/board (not a 47-page strategy deck)
