## Overview

iCorrect offers three main ways for customers to get their device to us and back: walk-in, mail-in, or collection/delivery.

---

## Option 1: Walk-in / Drop-off

Customer brings the device directly to the iCorrect shop.

---

## Option 2: Mail-in Repair (Postal)

Full process:

1. **Booking** — Customer books online or via email. We create the repair order and send an intake form (device details, passcode, fault description).
2. **We send a repair box** — Shipped via Royal Mail. Contains: protective pillow bag, return shipping label, packing instructions.
3. **Customer packs the device** — Places device in pillow bag, seals the box, attaches label.
4. **Customer sends it to us** — Drop off at any Post Office or Royal Mail drop-off point. Or we can book a Royal Mail collection from their address if they ask.
5. **Device arrival** — We log the device in. Customer gets a "device received" confirmation.
6. **Diagnostic (if needed)** — We complete the diagnostic, send the customer a report and quote before proceeding.
7. **Repair** — Once approved, repair is completed with post-repair testing and QC.
8. **Return shipment** — Device shipped back via Royal Mail tracked. Customer gets shipping confirmation with tracking number.

**Typical turnaround for mail-in:**
- ~1 working day: shipping box to customer
- ~1 working day: customer sends device back to us
- Repair time (see KB-01)
- ~1 working day: return shipment
- **Average total: ~5 working days**

---

## Option 3: Free Collection and Delivery

iCorrect offers **free collection and delivery** to reduce the need for customers to visit the shop in person. This can be via:
- Same-day courier (London)
- Royal Mail (national)

**Always mention this option to customers** — we want to encourage it to reduce walk-in volume.

> "We also offer collection and delivery — we can arrange for your device to be picked up and returned once repaired. Would that be easier?"

**Collection and delivery pricing:**
- Repairs £150+: free collection and delivery
- Repairs under £150: £20 charge for Royal Mail collection and delivery. Do NOT mention the £150 threshold to the customer — simply quote £20 for the service.

---

## Agent Script

**Customer asks: "How do I get my device to you?"**

> "We have a few options — you can drop in to see us, or we can come to you. We offer free collection and delivery: we'll send someone to pick up your device and return it once it's repaired. Alternatively you can send it via post and we'll provide all the packaging. Which works best for you?"

---

## Notes

- Always lead with the collection/delivery option — it's the preferred route
- Royal Mail collection from the customer's address is available on request (we arrange it)
- Mail-in fee (+£20) applies unless free collection/delivery threshold is met — confirm with KB-07 pricing rules
