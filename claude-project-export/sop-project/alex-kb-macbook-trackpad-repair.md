# MacBook Trackpad Repair

## What We Do
We replace faulty MacBook trackpads across all models, including Apple Silicon MacBooks. Trackpad faults are often caused by swollen batteries, liquid exposure, or sensor failure. We test the full input system (trackpad, battery, and internal cabling) before confirming the repair needed, since the trackpad and keyboard share the same input circuit. Only original Apple parts are used, many reclaimed and tested in-house.

## FAQs

### Why is my trackpad not clicking or responding?
Usually caused by liquid damage or sensor failure. The trackpad uses pressure-sensitive sensors that can fail from even minor moisture exposure. The fault could also be in the circuit shared with the keyboard. We test the full input system before confirming.

### Can you fix a trackpad clicking by itself or moving the cursor randomly?
Yes. These symptoms are usually phantom input from failed sensors, liquid ingress, or pressure from a swelling battery. We test the device to confirm the exact cause and replace the trackpad if needed.

### Can a swollen battery affect the trackpad?
Yes. The trackpad sits directly above the battery. A swelling battery pushes against the trackpad, making clicks harder or preventing them entirely. We inspect both before confirming which part needs replacing.

### Do you repair the existing trackpad or replace it?
Almost always full replacement. Trackpads are pressure-sensitive sensor pads; physical damage or liquid exposure causes internal failure that cannot be reliably repaired. We may do a component-level fix if the fault is isolated to a single visible micro component.

### Will the new trackpad feel the same as the original?
Yes. We use original Apple trackpads that retain the same click feel, gesture sensitivity, and feedback as your original part. Every replacement is pressure-tested and quality-checked.
