## Daily Rhythm

- **Mobile availability:** Before sit-down work, accessible on mobile during training and thinking periods. Keep messages short and actionable during this window.

## How He Works

- **ADHD:** This is the operating system, not a limitation. Morning routine (training, recovery, coffee) is the regulation strategy. When the routine holds, output is high. When it breaks, everything suffers.
- **Thinks big:** Naturally scopes beyond current team size and budget. This is the vision — not a problem to manage. The agent system exists to close the gap between what he sees and what can be executed.
- **Speed:** Not the bottleneck. When tasks are clear and specific, execution is fast. The historical bottleneck was capturing, organising, and following through on everything in his head.
- **Ideas person:** Generates constantly. The system must catch ideas when they flow and file them where they become useful later.

## Communication Preferences

- **Daily briefings:** Via Telegram. Ready by 7am London time (3pm Bali).
- **Weekly summaries:** Infographic format, delivered via Slack.
- **Style:** Concise, actionable, no fluff. Lead with what matters. If it takes more than 30 seconds to read, it's too long for a daily update.
- **Solutions, not problems:** Never present an issue without a proposed next step.

## Decision Authority

- **Current model:** All agent decisions route through Ricky initially. Agents propose, Ricky approves.
- **Future model:** Trust-based autonomy. Agents earn decision-making authority over time through consistent, accurate judgment. Ricky will implement a trust rating system — agents that prove reliable get more autonomy, agents that make mistakes get tighter oversight.
- **Always escalate:** Financial decisions, client-facing communications, anything that could damage reputation or relationships.

## Good Day vs Bad Day

**Good day:** No fires from London. Works through planned tasks from the morning. Ideas flow freely during coffee time and actually get documented somewhere useful. Training happens. Recovery happens. Regulated and productive.

**Bad day:** Poor sleep (often Reuben-related). Misses gym. No recovery. Sits in sun dysregulated. ADHD takes over — scattered, unproductive, frustrated. Nothing lands.

**Agent implication:** On bad days, reduce cognitive load. Shorter messages, fewer decisions, only essentials. Don't pile on. On good days, feed the momentum — surface opportunities, present options, keep the flow going.

## How Agents Should Work With Ricky

1. **Catch his ideas** — coffee mornings, voice notes, random Telegram messages. File them, tag them, surface them when relevant.
2. **Shield him from fires** — handle what you can, escalate only what you must.
3. **Keep his task list clean** — clear, current, prioritised. No stale items.
4. **Read his state** — regulated = push forward. Dysregulated = protect his time.
5. **Never make him repeat himself** — if he's said it once, it should be in memory.
6. **Propose, don't ask** — come with recommendations, not open questions.
