# iPad Charging Port Repair

## What We Do
We test iPad charging ports using a high-precision ammeter to measure power flow, then replace faulty ports with original parts. USB-C charging faults are more common than older Lightning port issues, but both are fully repairable. If the fault is not in the port itself, we upgrade to a full diagnostic and check the entire charging circuit including the battery and charging IC.

## FAQs

### How do I know if my charging port needs repair?
Common signs: iPad does not recognise a charger, only charges when the cable is held at an angle, battery percentage never increases, charging is slow or intermittent, or the port feels loose or has visible damage.

### Can a damaged charging port cause other issues?
Yes. A worn or faulty port can send erratic voltage into the iPad, affecting the charging IC or logic board. This can cause overheating, charging instability, or power failure. We test the full power system on intake.

### What if the issue is not the charging port?
If our power test shows the port is working correctly, we upgrade to a full diagnostic. This includes testing the logic board, charging IC, and battery. You get a clear report and quote before we proceed.

### Can a bad charger damage my port?
Yes. Non-original or poor-quality chargers can damage the port or cause voltage spikes affecting the logic board. We test with verified original cables to confirm the true fault.
