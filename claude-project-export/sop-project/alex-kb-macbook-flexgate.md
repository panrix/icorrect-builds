# MacBook Flexgate Repair

## What We Do
Flexgate is a design flaw in 2016-2018 MacBook Pro models (A1706, A1707, A1708) where the backlight flex cable was too short. Over time, opening and closing the lid frays the cable, killing the backlight. We repair by replacing and lengthening the flex cable under a microscope, reinforcing the weak point. Original display is preserved; no full screen replacement needed.

## FAQs

### How do I know if my MacBook has Flexgate?
Screen turns black when lid is opened past a certain angle, or a "stage light" glow appears at the bottom edge. Works only at certain angles. If you see pink/grey bars instead, that may be Dustgate (different fault).

### Can Flexgate be repaired without replacing the screen?
Yes. We replace and reinforce the backlight flex cable. The original Apple display is kept intact.

### Does Apple still offer free Flexgate repairs?
Apple offered free repairs for affected 13-inch models within six years of purchase. The programme did not cover 15-inch models and is no longer active for most devices. Worth checking with Apple first.

### Will Flexgate get worse over time?
Yes. The cable continues to fray with use. Eventually the screen may stop working entirely and can affect the camera too.

### My camera stopped working too. Is this related?
Possibly. The camera uses a flex cable alongside the backlight cable. Both can be affected by the same strain. We check both during every Flexgate repair.

### Can I fix Flexgate myself?
Not recommended. DIY cable extenders or angle adjustments are temporary fixes that often make the damage worse. Proper repair requires microscope work and correct soldering.
