## FAQs

### How do I know if my charging port needs repair?
Signs: only charges when cable is angled, MagSafe LED not lighting, USB-C ports feel loose, slow or inconsistent charging, issue persists with a different original charger.

### Can you repair both USB-C and MagSafe ports?
Yes. Both types across all MacBook Air and Pro models, from Intel to Apple Silicon.

### What if the issue is not the port?
We test with a high-accuracy ammeter. If the port is fine, we upgrade to a diagnostic and test the charging IC, logic board, and battery input. We repair at component level rather than replacing the whole board.

### Can a faulty port damage the logic board?
Yes. A corroded or shorting port can send irregular voltage into the board, damaging USB-C ICs or power delivery components. Early repair prevents worse damage.

### Can you repair a port damaged by liquid?
Yes, handled as a liquid damage diagnostic. We isolate corrosion, clean and test the board, and only return the MacBook when restored to safe working order.

### How do I know if the issue is my charger?
Test with another original Apple charger. If the issue disappears, the charger is at fault. Third-party or imitation chargers can deliver unstable voltage and damage USB-C controllers.
