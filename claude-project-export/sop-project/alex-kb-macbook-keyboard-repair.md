## FAQs

### Why is my MacBook typing double letters or missing keystrokes?
Usually caused by minor liquid damage to the keyboard circuit. Even small amounts of moisture can disrupt signal. The correct fix is full keyboard replacement.

### Can you fix a single key?
No. Single key replacement is a patch fix. There is always a reason the key failed, usually linked to internal circuitry. Full keyboard replacement prevents repeat problems.

### Apple quoted me for a full top case. Can you just replace the keyboard?
Yes. Apple replaces the full top case (keyboard, trackpad, battery). We replace just the keyboard using original parts, at less than half the cost.

### My keys feel crunchy or sticky. Can that be repaired?
Yes, but start with a diagnostic. Sticky keys usually indicate liquid under the keyboard or backlight. If corrosion is found, we check the rest of the system first.

### Can liquid on the keyboard damage other components?
Yes. On newer models, liquid entering around the arrow keys can seep into the logic board and damage the audio circuit. Book a full diagnostic to confirm the extent.

### Do you repair butterfly keyboard issues?
Yes. We recommend checking with Apple first; they offer a free repair programme for eligible 2016-2019 models (but turnaround can be up to 10 working days). We can complete it in 1-3 working days.
