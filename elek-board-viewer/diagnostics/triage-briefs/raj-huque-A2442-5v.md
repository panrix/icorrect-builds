## Diagnosis summary

**This board is stuck at 5V -- the CD3217 USB-C controller isn't negotiating Power Delivery to 20V.** But crucially, **DFU mode connects** -- which means the CD3217 IS communicating over USB data. The PD negotiation for voltage is failing.

The A2442 `dcps_5v_triage.json` flow places 5V/0.090A in the **0.05-0.2A (partial boot attempt)** scenario:
> *"Moderate current. PP3V8_AON likely present but downstream short holding board down."*

**The fluctuating PP5V_S2_MAIN and PP1V2_S2 rails are the key diagnostic signal.** These are post-boot rails that should NOT be active at 5V:
- **PP5V_S2_MAIN** is generated by a buck converter -- it's a Stage 2 (S2) rail that only comes up after the PMU enables it. Confirmed on U9300 (Viper VR) among other components.
- **PP1V2_S2** is another S2 rail

If they're fluctuating, the boot sequence is **repeatedly starting and failing** -- the PMU tries to bring up S2 rails, something faults, and it shuts down, then tries again. This is a micro-cycling pattern too fast to see on the ammeter (which just shows "up and down").

**Why U9300 (Viper VR) replacement didn't fix it:**
Viper is a voltage regulator for the SoC DVFS domain. If the fluctuation is on PP5V_S2_MAIN (upstream of Viper), replacing Viper won't help -- the fault is earlier in the chain. The TPS62130 or its enable signal is the more likely culprit. U9300 is confirmed to carry PP5V_S2_MAIN, which validates it as the correct target for Saf's replacement -- the problem is just upstream of it.

## Step 1 — Forced-start bypass (MASTER TRIAGE SHORTCUT)

**This is the fastest way to isolate front-end vs. back-end fault.** Source: Eason (iphone_cpu_fix) via MASTER-TRIAGE-FLOW.

Instead of walking the full power chain step by step, we bypass the front end entirely and inject power directly into the back end. If the board boots -> fault is front-end (CD3217 PD negotiation, charger IC, 20V boost). If not -> fault is back-end (PMU, SoC, clocks).

**Procedure for A2442:**

1. **Safety check FIRST:** Measure PP3V8_AON_VDDMAIN resistance to ground in diode mode.
   - `PP3V8_AON_VDDMAIN to GND: ___Ohm`
   - If near-zero -> **STOP. Dead short on PP3V8_AON. Find and fix it before injecting anything.**
2. **Connect bench PSU at 3.8V** (use 4.3V if display is connected) **to PP3V8_AON_VDDMAIN**
3. **Force PMU_VDD_HI high** -- on A2442 this uses a **summing amplifier** on the "AON Output Throttle" schematic page (not a simple pin jumper like A2337).
4. **Press start button OR connect charger for 3 seconds**

**Reply to Ricky with:**

| Result | Meaning | Next step |
|---|---|---|
| **Board boots** (fan spins, display lights, Apple logo) | FRONT END fault. The back-end (PMU, SoC, NAND) is healthy. The problem is in the CD3217 -> charger -> 20V boost path. | -> Go to Step 5 (CD3217 PD negotiation check) |
| **Board does not boot** (no fan, no display, nothing) | BACK END fault. Even with direct 3.8V, the PMU/SoC won't start. The fluctuating PP5V_S2 is a symptom of a back-end issue. | -> Go to Step 2 (power chain walkdown) |
| **Board partially boots** (fan spins briefly, then dies) | Back-end instability. PMU starts but can't sustain. Likely the same cause as the PP5V_S2 fluctuation. | -> Go to Step 3 (focus on PP5V_S2 source) |

`Forced start result: BOOTS / DOES NOT BOOT / PARTIAL (describe: ___)`

## Step 3 — Focus on the fluctuating rail

Based on Saf's Mar 2025 notes, **PP5V_S2_MAIN** is fluctuating. If confirmed:

1. **Check the TPS62130** (or equivalent 5V S2 buck on A2442)
   - Is it getting its enable signal? Probe `P5VS2_PWR_EN`
   - `P5VS2_PWR_EN: ___V -- stable / fluctuating / missing`
2. **If enable is stable but output fluctuates:**
   - TPS62130 may be damaged, or its output has a downstream short that's loading it
   - **Remove the TPS62130 output inductor** and measure current draw on the rail -- does the fluctuation stop?
   - `Without inductor: fluctuation stops / continues`
3. **If enable is also fluctuating:**
   - The PMU is cycling the enable -- something downstream is faulting and the PMU is retrying
   - Thermal camera during the fluctuation -- what's getting warm?
   - `Hot component: ___`

## Step 4 — PP1V2_S2 investigation

PP1V2_S2 is the other fluctuating rail. This could be:
- **Caused by** PP5V_S2_MAIN fluctuating (if PP1V2_S2 is derived from PP5V_S2_MAIN, it'll follow)
- **Independent** -- a separate short on the 1.2V S2 rail

**After Step 3 (once PP5V_S2_MAIN is understood):**

1. **If PP5V_S2_MAIN is fixed and PP1V2_S2 stabilizes too** -> they were linked. Done.
2. **If PP5V_S2_MAIN is fixed but PP1V2_S2 still fluctuates** -> independent fault on the 1.2V rail
   - Check the PP1V2_S2 buck converter
   - Diode mode on PP1V2_S2 output
   - `PP1V2_S2 diode: ___`

---

## Step 5 — If board still won't negotiate 20V after rails are stable

If all S2 rails are present and stable but the board still sits at 5V:

1. **Check CD3217 PD negotiation:**
   - The CD3217 reads its ROM (SPI NOR) to know the PD profile
   - If ROM is corrupted -> CD3217 never requests 20V from the charger
   - Try re-programming the CD3217 ROM, or swap CD3217 from a donor
2. **Check the charger IC (U5200) I2C communication:**
   - The SoC tells U5200 to accept 20V input via I2C
   - If SoC never boots far enough to send this command, the charger stays at 5V
   - This circles back to why the boot is stalling
