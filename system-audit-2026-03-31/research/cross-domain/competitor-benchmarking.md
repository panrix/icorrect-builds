## Scope

## Short Answer

- `Observed`: iCorrect’s published entry pricing is not obviously out of market.
- `Observed`: the London market splits into two visible clusters:
  - high-volume convenience chains and local fast-turn shops
  - Mac-specialist premium-diagnosis operators
- `Observed`: several competitors promise same-day or near-same-day repairs, so turnaround is part of the competitive baseline, not a differentiator by itself.
- `Inferred`: current business losses are unlikely to be explained by iCorrect simply pricing too high versus the market.
- `Inferred`: the bigger risk is failing to convert and fulfil demand efficiently enough while competitors offer similar speed claims and broadly comparable list pricing.

## 1. iCorrect Positioning

- `Observed`: iCorrect’s public pricing starts at:
  - MacBook from `£49`
  - iPhone from `£39`
  - iPad from `£49`
  - Apple Watch from `£29`
- `Observed`: the iCorrect page leans on:
  - upfront quotes

- `Observed`: operator confirmation says the core USP to preserve in competitor comparison is that iCorrect can complete advanced repairs that many competitors cannot.

## 2. Competitor Snapshot

### iSmash

- `Observed`: iSmash positions as a large-chain Apple/Samsung/Google repair brand with express service and mail-in options.
- `Observed`: public Apple pricing summary shows:
  - iPhone screen repairs from `£35` to `£175`
  - MacBook battery replacements from `£79.99` to `£139.99`
  - iPad repairs from `£20` to `£449`
- `Observed`: iSmash states most iPhone repairs take between `30 minutes` and `1 hour`.
- `Observed`: public Trustpilot surface shows:
  - TrustScore `4.0`
  - `16,019` total reviews
- `Inferred`: iSmash competes on convenience, footprint, and social proof more than specialist depth.

### Mac Repair London

- `Observed`: Mac Repair London positions as a Mac-focused central London specialist with multiple drop-off points.
- `Observed`: public pricing examples are premium/specialist style:
  - MacBook SSD upgrades around `£250-460 + VAT`
  - labour commonly `£95 + VAT`
  - cleaning service from `£95 + VAT`
- `Observed`: public pages state many repairs are completed the same day.
- `Inferred`: this is a specialist-Mac competitor, not a broad value-led Apple-repair chain.

### Mind The Mac

- `Observed`: Mind The Mac positions as a small London Apple specialist covering Mac, iPhone, and iPad.
- `Observed`: public pricing includes:
  - iPhone 6 LCD replacement `£39.99`
  - iPhone 6s Plus LCD replacement `£59.99`
  - Mac logic-board repairs from `£99.99`
  - keyboard replacements from `£109.99`
  - glass/screen replacements from `£79.99`
  - liquid damage repairs from `£99.99`
- `Observed`: Mind The Mac is weekday-only in the current visible opening-hours surface.
- `Inferred`: Mind The Mac competes as a smaller specialist with older-style transparent menu pricing, but less obvious scale and convenience than a chain.

### I Repair Macs

- `Observed`: I Repair Macs positions as a premium small-business Apple specialist, explicitly not trying to be the cheapest.
- `Observed`: public pricing signals include:
  - MacBook diagnosis fee `£79`
  - liquid damage diagnosis `£139`
  - MacBook Pro 13\" A1706 battery replacement `£180-230`
- `Observed`: same-day appointments are described as available `99%` of the time, with battery and screen jobs often within `24 hours` when parts are in hand.
- `Observed`: public Trustpilot surface shows:
  - TrustScore `4.9`
  - `81` total reviews
- `Inferred`: I Repair Macs is positioned as a premium trust-and-specialism play, not a price leader.

### TommyTech

- `Observed`: TommyTech positions as a local West London convenience-led repair shop covering phones, MacBooks, and other electronics.
- `Observed`: public iPhone screen pricing includes:
  - iPhone 6 to 8 Plus `£25-40`
  - iPhone X/XS `£70`
  - iPhone 11 / 11 Pro `£80`
  - iPhone 14 `£130`
  - iPhone 15 Pro Max / Plus `£200`
- `Observed`: TommyTech says most screen replacements are done the same day and advertises a `3-month` warranty.
- `Observed`: the site claims `336+` Google reviews.
- `Inferred`: TommyTech competes aggressively on convenience and visible headline price, especially on common iPhone repairs.

## 3. Benchmark Interpretation

- `Observed`: iCorrect’s entry-price framing is broadly competitive with London peers and in some cases cheaper than specialist Mac operators.
- `Observed`: same-day turnaround is widely claimed across competitors, so fast service is a baseline expectation.
- `Observed`: several competitors make trust/reputation highly legible through review volume, convenience messaging, or explicit specialist positioning.
- `Inferred`: if iCorrect is underperforming, the likely explanation is not simply “the website price list is too expensive”.
- `Inferred`: the more plausible gap is execution:
  - weaker lead conversion
  - slower or more fragmented follow-up
  - operational drag after the sale
  - weaker market signalling of convenience/trust than the strongest public competitors

## 4. Working Conclusion

- `Observed`: the public market does not currently make iCorrect look structurally uncompetitive on headline price.
- `Inferred`: the business still has room to win if it tightens:
  - response speed
  - conversion path clarity
  - workshop throughput
  - trust signalling around reviews, speed, and specialist capability
- `Inferred`: competitor comparison should not be reduced to cheap-versus-expensive alone; the more defensible comparison basket is advanced MacBook diagnostic/no-power work plus original-screen iPhone repair, because that is closer to iCorrect's actual edge.
