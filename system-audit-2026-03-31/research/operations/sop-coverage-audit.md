# SOP Coverage Audit

Date: 2026-04-02

## Scope and method

- This audit uses only the source set requested by the brief.
- `SOP exists?` means:
  - `yes`: a real SOP or equivalent operational procedure document exists
  - `partial`: only a thin KB note, journey doc, data-flow note, or spec exists
  - `no`: no written procedure was found in the audited source set
- `Current status` means:
  - `enforced`: the procedure is both written and evidenced as active/live
  - `written but not enforced`: documented, but still manual/thin/on-demand/not fully controlled
  - `draft`: spec/pilot/proposed design, not current operating truth
  - `stale`: contradicted by newer live evidence or tied to retired ownership/runtime
  - `missing`: no procedure found
- Owner fields marked `[ASSUMPTION]` are inferred from `team-operations-summary.md` when the source set does not name an owner directly.
- For `partial` rows, the `SOP location` column gives the nearest written process document, not a fully valid SOP.

## Overall conclusion

- Back Market is the only area with a substantial SOP corpus, but even there the estate is incomplete: SOP 05 is missing, listing and buy-box still depend on manual/runtime splits, and returns/aftercare remain only weakly systematised.
- Outside Back Market, the business does not have a reliable company-wide SOP layer for intake, diagnostics, quoting, payment closure, phone lead promotion, or customer-wait follow-up.
- The highest-priority SOP build tracks are:
  - company-wide intake
  - quote / approval / stale-exception handling
  - payment write-back and reconciliation
  - QC checklist plus BM SOP 05
  - phone lead promotion and response SLA
  - queue management and front-desk ownership
