# Back Market Trade-In Journey

Status: rebuilt from verified docs/code/data on 2026-04-02

## Scope And Ground Truth

- This journey is the seller-side BM buyback path: customer sells a device to iCorrect through Back Market.
- The live BM ingress path is `sent-orders.js`, not the older inactive n8n flows.
- Core BM automation now lives in VPS services: `icloud-checker` (`8010`), `bm-grade-check` (`8011`), `bm-payout` (`8012`), and later BM resale/shipping services.
- The strongest currently profitable growth signal identified by Ricky is the non-functioning-device BM trade-in model.

## Journey Map

1. Customer accepts a Back Market trade-in offer
- Customer touchpoint: BM buyback listing / checkout on Back Market, not on iCorrect’s own website.
- Internal handoff: the seller’s order enters iCorrect’s BM trade-in acquisition flow once BM marks it `SENT`.
- Systems involved: Back Market buyback platform; BM API.
- Automation state: automated on BM’s side; iCorrect reacts only after BM state changes.
- Delay points and failure modes: no iCorrect-side case exists until BM reaches the `SENT` state and the scheduled ingestion job runs.
- Sources: `/home/ricky/builds/backmarket/sops/00-BACK-MARKET-MASTER.md`; `/home/ricky/builds/backmarket/sops/01-trade-in-purchase.md`.

2. `sent-orders.js` creates linked Monday items
- Customer touchpoint: customer does not see this directly.
- Internal handoff: BM order becomes visible to operations through one Main Board item and one BM Devices item.
- Systems involved: `scripts/sent-orders.js`; BM API; Monday Main Board `349212843`; BM Devices Board `3892194968`; Telegram alerting.
- Automation state: scheduled automation, live cron at `06:00 UTC`.
- Delay points and failure modes: if the BM API fetch fails, the run exits and alerts. If one board item is created and the other fails, the order is left partially ingested and needs manual follow-up. The current script de-dups on the Main Board BM trade-in ID, which differs from older n8n behavior.
- Sources: `/home/ricky/builds/backmarket/sops/01-trade-in-purchase.md`; `/home/ricky/builds/backmarket/README.md`; `findings.md`.

3. Customer ships the device to iCorrect
- Customer touchpoint: customer uses the BM packing-slip / logistics path generated by Back Market.
- Internal handoff: none inside iCorrect yet beyond awaiting receipt.
- Systems involved: BM order detail and transfer certificate / packing slip; Monday `Incoming Future` / `Expecting Device`.
- Automation state: customer-side shipping is outside iCorrect’s direct automation.
- Delay points and failure modes: if the parcel arrives but staff cannot match it to the existing Monday item, the journey stalls before serial entry and downstream checks.
- Sources: `/home/ricky/builds/backmarket/sops/01-trade-in-purchase.md`; `/home/ricky/builds/backmarket/sops/02-intake-receiving.md`.

4. Staff receives the parcel and enters the serial number
- Customer touchpoint: none directly, unless an exception is detected later.
- Internal handoff: intake/front desk or workshop receiving hands the device into the automated validation path.
- Systems involved: Monday Main Board `status4 = Received`; `date4`; serial field `text4`.
- Automation state: parcel matching and serial entry are manual; entering `text4` triggers the webhook automatically.
- Delay points and failure modes: SOP 03 identifies missing arrival-alert automation and says moving items from `Incoming Future` to `Today's Repairs` is still manual. Intake coverage is therefore partly dependent on human vigilance.
- Sources: `/home/ricky/builds/backmarket/sops/02-intake-receiving.md`; `/home/ricky/builds/backmarket/sops/03-diagnostic.md`.

5. iCloud check and spec validation run
- Customer touchpoint: only exception cases are customer-visible at this stage.
- Internal handoff: serial entry hands the case from manual receiving to the `icloud-checker` service.
- Systems involved: `icloud-checker`; SICKW; Apple Self Service Repair API; Monday; BM Devices relation; Slack.
- Automation state: automated after serial entry.
- Delay points and failure modes: SickW failure stops the flow and posts an error comment. Apple spec lookup can fail without fully blocking the iCloud path. Missing BM Devices linkage prevents clean spec comparison.
- Sources: `/home/ricky/builds/backmarket/sops/02-intake-receiving.md`; `data_flows.md`; `platform_inventory/vps-webhooks.md`.

6. Exception branch: iCloud lock
- Customer touchpoint: seller receives BM message asking them to remove iCloud / Find My; they may trigger a recheck loop through their reply.
- Internal handoff: `icloud-checker` moves the item to the iCloud locked group and creates Slack alerts / recheck tasks.
- Systems involved: BM messages API; Monday iCloud status and group move; Slack; in-process recheck scheduler.
- Automation state: mostly automated once the lock is detected, with human review via Slack where needed.
- Delay points and failure modes: unresolved iCloud lock is a hard stop. The item can sit in locked validation until the seller responds or removes the lock. Alerts are deduped with a 6-hour cooldown, which reduces noise but can also reduce repeated visibility.
- Sources: `/home/ricky/builds/backmarket/sops/02-intake-receiving.md`; `platform_inventory/vps-webhooks.md`.

7. Exception branch: spec mismatch / counter-offer
- Customer touchpoint: seller receives a BM counter-offer only when the team decides the received device is worse than expected and the rate limit allows it.
- Internal handoff: automated mismatch detection hands off to Ricky/team decision-making through Slack buttons.
- Systems involved: `icloud-checker`; Monday `status24 = Counteroffer`; BM counter-offer API; Slack action buttons.
- Automation state: detection is automated; counter-offer submission is human-approved via Slack action.
- Delay points and failure modes: counter-offers are rate-limited to stay below `15%`. If the rate limit is reached, the business absorbs the mismatch. Customer has `7` days to accept/reject, and that wait can freeze payout.
- Sources: `/home/ricky/builds/backmarket/sops/02-intake-receiving.md`; `/home/ricky/builds/backmarket/sops/12-returns-aftercare.md`; `data_flows.md`.

8. Roni / BM diagnostics capture real condition and parts
- Customer touchpoint: none unless a later counter-offer or rejection is needed.
- Internal handoff: validated intake moves into BM diagnostics, currently owned by the BM diag role and strongly associated with Roni.
- Systems involved: Monday pre-grade, ammeter, reported-vs-actual, parts-required, intake notes, BM diag fields.
- Automation state: manual diagnostic capture; automated profitability check only after the status change to `Diagnostic Complete`.
- Delay points and failure modes: missing parts-required data weakens profitability accuracy. Roni is a cross-team bottleneck because he spans BM diagnostics, QC, and parts.
- Sources: `/home/ricky/builds/backmarket/sops/03-diagnostic.md`; `team-operations-summary.md`.

9. Profitability check runs on `Diagnostic Complete`
- Customer touchpoint: none directly.
- Internal handoff: diagnostic completion triggers an automated margin check before more labour is committed.
- Systems involved: `bm-grade-check`; Monday; sell-price dataset from `buyback-monitor`; Slack; Monday comments.
- Automation state: automated webhook after status change.
- Delay points and failure modes: if both pre-grades are not present, the service exits and waits for a later valid trigger. Unprofitable devices do not auto-stop the job; they create Slack warnings and comments, so action still depends on humans.
- Sources: `/home/ricky/builds/backmarket/sops/03-diagnostic.md`; `platform_inventory/vps-webhooks.md`.

10. Team manually marks the item `Pay-Out`
- Customer touchpoint: none directly.
- Internal handoff: human workshop/QC decision hands the item into the irreversible payout service.
- Systems involved: Monday `status24 = Pay-Out`.
- Automation state: manual trigger, automated execution.
- Delay points and failure modes: if staff trigger payout too early or without proper review, the webhook guards against some conditions but not all. Historical n8n auto-payout was disabled precisely because team-trigger mistakes were causing problems.
- Sources: `/home/ricky/builds/backmarket/sops/03b-trade-in-payout.md`; `platform_inventory/vps-webhooks.md`.

11. `bm-payout` validates the order and pays the seller
- Customer touchpoint: the seller receives BM-side payout after iCorrect validates the buyback order.
- Internal handoff: Monday status change hands off to the payout service and, on success, back to Monday as `Purchased`.
- Systems involved: `bm-payout`; Monday; BM buyback validate endpoint; Slack; Telegram.
- Automation state: automated once the status is set and the hard gates pass.
- Delay points and failure modes: missing BM trade-in ID or iCloud lock blocks payout. The service does not validate purchase-price equality or a separate spec-approved flag, so some business controls remain outside the service. A failed Monday write after successful BM validation leaves the business financially committed but operationally out of sync.
- Sources: `/home/ricky/builds/backmarket/sops/03b-trade-in-payout.md`; `data_flows.md`.

12. Device becomes iCorrect-owned inventory and hands off to refurb / resale
- Customer touchpoint: seller-side journey is effectively complete after payout unless there is a counter-offer or iCloud exception.
- Internal handoff: owned device moves from BM trade-in acquisition into repair/refurb/QC and later the BM resale listing journey.
- Systems involved: Monday `Purchased`, `To List`, later BM resale toolchain.
- Automation state: mixed. Ownership change is automated at payout, but repair/refurb and later listing publication still involve manual and operator-gated steps.
- Delay points and failure modes: listing publication is not fully scheduled; the business can pay out promptly but still trap capital in refurb or `To List` queues later.
- Sources: `/home/ricky/builds/backmarket/sops/03b-trade-in-payout.md`; `/home/ricky/builds/backmarket/sops/06-listing.md`; `findings.md`.

## Communication Gaps

- The seller mostly hears from iCorrect only when an exception occurs: iCloud lock, counter-offer, or BM-side messaging. There is no evidenced proactive iCorrect status cadence for the normal “received -> diagnosed -> paid” path.
- Arrival matching, movement into `Today's Repairs`, and some queue visibility remain manual, so silent internal stalls are possible before the seller ever hears anything.
- Counter-offer waiting time is structurally opaque because the customer has up to `7` days to respond and the system does not expose a rich owner dashboard inside this audit.

## Delay Points

- iCloud lock is the hardest stop.
- Spec mismatch / counter-offer introduces a long external wait that the workshop cannot resolve unilaterally.
- Roni concentration across diagnostics, QC, and parts creates a shared bottleneck in the BM path.
- Profitability warnings still rely on human reaction; unprofitable devices can continue consuming time if nobody acts on the Slack alert.

## Improvement Opportunities

- Build an explicit seller-facing status model for BM trade-ins so “received”, “under assessment”, “counter-offer waiting”, and “paid out” are internally visible and owner-assigned.
- Automate the arrival-alert and auto-move steps called out as missing in SOP 03.
- Add a stronger payout checklist surface before `Pay-Out` so operator mistakes do not depend only on service-side hard gates.
- Track counter-offer ageing and iCloud-lock ageing in Monday with clear owners and escalation rules.
